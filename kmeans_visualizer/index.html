How K-Means Works

K

What is K?

K is the number of clusters you want to find in your data. You choose K before the algorithm starts. Each cluster groups data points that are close to each other in space.

✕

What is a Centroid?

A centroid (shown as ✕) is the center of a cluster. It begins at a random position and is updated each iteration to sit at the average position of all points assigned to it.

↻

Each Iteration

Step 1: Assign each point to its nearest centroid using Euclidean distance.

Step 2: Move each centroid to the average (x̄, ȳ) of its assigned points.

Why K Matters

Too few clusters miss real structure. Too many may overfit noise. The Elbow Method plots variance vs K — the "elbow" point is often the best K for your data.
